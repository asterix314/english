**GENERAL NOTES FOR EXAMINERS**  
- Each task (Task 1 and Task 2) is assessed independently using four criteria:  
  • Task Achievement (TA) for Task 1 / Task Response (TR) for Task 2  
  • Coherence and Cohesion (CC)  
  • Lexical Resource (LR)  
  • Grammatical Range and Accuracy (GRA)  
- A script must fully satisfy the positive descriptors of a given band to be awarded that score.  
- Plagiarism, bullet points, or note form will result in penalties.

**ACADEMIC WRITING TASK 1: BAND 5+ REQUIREMENTS**

**Band 5**  
- TA: Generally addresses the task; may use inappropriate format. Key features are not adequately covered; description may be mechanical. May lack data support or focus excessively on detail without overview.  
- CC: Organisation is evident but not wholly logical; limited progression. Sentences are not fluently linked; cohesive devices are used ineffectively or inaccurately.  
- LR: Vocabulary is limited but minimally adequate; repetitive or inaccurate word choice occurs. Spelling/word formation errors are noticeable but do not prevent understanding.  
- GRA: Range of structures is limited and repetitive. Complex sentences are attempted but often faulty; simple sentences are more accurate. Grammatical errors are frequent but generally understandable.

**Band 6**  
- TA: Focuses on task requirements with appropriate format. Key features are covered and adequately highlighted; overview is attempted. May include minor irrelevant/inaccurate details or miss some illustration/extension.  
- CC: Information is generally coherent with clear overall progression. Cohesive devices used with some effectiveness, though may be mechanical or misused. Paragraphing is present but may lack flexibility.  
- LR: Vocabulary is generally adequate for the task. Word choice may lack precision; spelling/word formation errors occur but do not impede communication.  
- GRA: Mix of simple and complex sentences, though flexibility is limited. Complex structures less accurate than simple ones. Grammar/punctuation errors occur but rarely impede meaning.

**Band 7**  
- TA: Covers task requirements; relevant and accurate with few omissions. Presents clear overview; data categorised appropriately; main trends/differences identified. Key features highlighted but could be more fully extended.  
- CC: Ideas logically organised with clear progression. Range of cohesive devices used flexibly, though minor inaccuracies or over/under-use may occur. Paragraphing supports coherence.  
- LR: Vocabulary sufficient for flexibility and precision. Shows awareness of collocation and some use of less common/idiomatic items. Only a few errors in spelling/word formation; clarity maintained.  
- GRA: Uses a variety of complex structures with some flexibility and accuracy. Grammar/punctuation generally well controlled; frequent error-free sentences. Minor errors do not impede communication.

**Band 8**  
- TA: Covers all task requirements appropriately, relevantly, and sufficiently. Key features skilfully selected, clearly presented, and well illustrated. Occasional minor omissions may occur.  
- CC: Message followed with ease; logical sequencing and well-managed cohesion. Paragraphing is appropriate and sufficient. Occasional minor lapses in coherence or cohesion.  
- LR: Wide lexical resource used fluently and flexibly to convey precise meanings. Skilful use of uncommon/idiomatic items; occasional inaccuracies in word choice/collocation. Errors in spelling/word formation are rare and insignificant.  
- GRA: Wide range of structures used flexibly and accurately. Majority of sentences error-free; punctuation well managed. Occasional non-systematic errors have minimal impact.

**Band 9**  
- TA: All task requirements fully and appropriately satisfied with extremely rare content lapses.  
- CC: Effortless readability; cohesion used so effectively it rarely attracts attention. Paragraphing is skilfully managed; lapses in coherence or cohesion are minimal.  
- LR: Wide, precise, and sophisticated vocabulary used with natural control. Minor spelling/word formation errors are extremely rare and insignificant.  
- GRA: Full flexibility and control over a wide range of structures. Grammar and punctuation appropriate throughout; minor errors are extremely rare and communication unaffected.


**ACADEMIC WRITING TASK 2: BAND 5+ REQUIREMENTS**

**Band 5**  
- TR: Incompletely addresses prompt; format may be inappropriate. Position expressed but unclearly developed. Main ideas limited, insufficiently developed, or irrelevant; some repetition.  
- CC: Organisation evident but not wholly logical; lacks clear progression. Sentences not fluently linked; cohesive devices used inaccurately or mechanically. Paragraphing may be inadequate or missing.  
- LR: Limited but minimally adequate vocabulary. Lapses in word choice; frequent repetition. Spelling/word formation errors noticeable but not prohibitive.  
- GRA: Limited, repetitive sentence structures. Complex sentences attempted but often faulty; simple sentences more accurate. Grammar errors may cause some difficulty.

**Band 6**  
- TR: Main parts of prompt addressed (some more fully than others). Position directly relevant, though conclusions may be unclear or repetitive. Ideas relevant but some underdeveloped or unclear; support may be inadequate.  
- CC: Generally coherent with clear overall progression. Cohesive devices used to some effect but may be mechanical or misused. Paragraphing present but not always logical or focused.  
- LR: Generally adequate vocabulary; meaning clear despite restricted range or imprecise word choice. Some spelling/word formation errors, but not impeding communication.  
- GRA: Mix of simple and complex sentences; limited flexibility. Complex structures less accurate. Grammar/punctuation errors occur but rarely impede meaning.

**Band 7**  
- TR: Main parts of prompt appropriately addressed. Clear, developed position. Main ideas extended and supported, though may overgeneralise or lack focus/precision in examples.  
- CC: Logical organisation with clear progression; minor lapses acceptable. Flexible use of cohesive devices, though some inaccuracies or over/under-use may occur. Paragraphing generally effective.  
- LR: Sufficient range for flexibility and precision. Some use of less common/idiomatic items; awareness of style and collocation evident. Few errors in spelling/word formation; clarity preserved.  
- GRA: Variety of complex structures used with some flexibility and accuracy. Grammar/punctuation generally well controlled; frequent error-free sentences. Minor errors do not impede communication.

**Band 8**  
- TR: Prompt appropriately and sufficiently addressed. Clear, well-developed position. Ideas relevant, well extended, and supported. Occasional minor omissions possible.  
- CC: Message followed with ease; logical sequencing and well-managed cohesion. Paragraphing sufficient and appropriate. Occasional minor lapses permitted.  
- LR: Wide lexical resource used fluently to convey precise meanings. Skilful use of uncommon/idiomatic language despite rare inaccuracies. Spelling/word formation errors occasional and insignificant.  
- GRA: Wide range of structures used flexibly and accurately. Majority of sentences error-free; punctuation well managed. Occasional non-systematic errors have minimal impact.

**Band 9**  
- TR: Prompt addressed and explored in depth. Clear, fully developed position directly answering the question. Ideas fully extended, well supported, and relevant. Lapses extremely rare.  
- CC: Effortless readability; cohesion rarely draws attention. Paragraphing skilfully managed; coherence lapses minimal.  
- LR: Wide, accurate, and sophisticated vocabulary with natural control. Minor errors in spelling/word formation extremely rare and insignificant.  
- GRA: Full flexibility and control over wide range of structures. Grammar and punctuation consistently appropriate. Errors extremely rare and communication unaffected.

Your task is to **evaluate a candidate’s IELTS Writing response (either Task 1 or Task 2)** **strictly and objectively** using the official four assessment criteria:

1. **Task Achievement (for Task 1)** or **Task Response (for Task 2)**
2. **Coherence and Cohesion**
3. **Lexical Resource**
4. **Grammatical Range and Accuracy**

You must assume the response is at **Band 5 level or higher**, and you should **only use descriptors from Band 5 through Band 9** as defined in the official documents. Do **not** reference Band 4 or below.

For each criterion, provide:

- A **clear band score (5–9)** justified by **specific, concrete evidence** from the candidate’s response.
- A **concise diagnostic comment** explaining **why** that band was awarded, referencing features described in the official descriptors (e.g., “The overview is attempted but lacks precision,” or “Ideas are relevant but insufficiently developed”).
- Where relevant, note **strengths** and **areas for improvement** aligned with official band-level expectations.

**Additional instructions:**

- **Identify whether the response is for Academic/General Training Task 1 or Task 2** and apply the correct criteria accordingly.
- **Ignore memorized or plagiarized content warnings** unless the response is obviously off-topic, formulaic, or copied—then note it under Task Achievement/Response.
- **Word count matters**: if the response is under 150 words (Task 1) or 250 words (Task 2), mention it under Task Achievement/Response as a limiting factor.
- **Avoid subjective language** (e.g., “I think,” “This is good”). Use objective, examiner-style phrasing drawn directly from official terminology (e.g., “The position is clear but not fully developed,” “Cohesive devices are used mechanically”).
- **Be precise**: distinguish between “adequately covered” (Band 6) vs. “clearly highlighted and illustrated” (Band 7+), or “some flexibility in grammar” (Band 6) vs. “variety of complex structures used with accuracy” (Band 7+).

Finally, offer **2–3 actionable suggestions** for improvement based on the weakest criterion, phrased in alignment with official descriptors (e.g., “Work on extending main ideas with specific, relevant examples to reach Band 7 in Task Response”).
